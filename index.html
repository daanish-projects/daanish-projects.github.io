About Me

I am a researcher at One Text Initiative, where I conduct qualitative and quantitative research on political systems, governance, and electoral dynamics in Sri Lanka. My work involves extensive data collection, analysis, and the development of evidence-based research reports that contribute to understanding complex political phenomena.

My research portfolio includes studies on political clientelism, parliamentary re-election patterns, decentralized budgeting, and environmental policy analysis.

Research Projects

Selected research contributions and analytical studies

Research Details

Parliamentary Re-election Rates Analysis (1984-2025)

Analysis of re-election patterns of parliamentary members in Sri Lanka over four decades, examining trends in political continuity, voter behavior, and electoral dynamics through quantitative data analysis.

Quantitative Research Data Analysis Political Science Electoral Studies
View Document

Political Clientelism in Sri Lanka: A Snapshot

Research examining patron-client relationships in Sri Lankan politics through multiple analytical lenses. This multi-method study included extensive qualitative fieldwork on sandmining and political patronage networks, alongside a longitudinal quantitative analysis of national decentralized budgets (1974-2023), culminating in a detailed research report analyzing the mechanisms, prevalence, and impact of political clientelism on governance and democratic institutions.

Qualitative Research Quantitative Research Political Analysis Governance Multi-Method Study
Research Details

Presidential Elections Voting Patterns Analysis (1982-2019)

Research on presidential elections from 1982 to 2019 analyzing voting patterns based on districts, divisions and ethnicities. This study examines electoral behavior, demographic influences, and spatial voting trends across Sri Lanka's presidential election history.

Quantitative Research Data Analysis Electoral Studies Political Science Demographics

Parliament MP Data Scraper

Python-based web scraper that extracts MP information from the parliament website, including names, political parties, districts, sitting and non-sitting contact details, and office addresses. Streamlines data collection for research and analysis by automating the extraction of key information across all parliamentary members.
